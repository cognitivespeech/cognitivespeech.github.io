Abstract

In this paper, we present the Microsoft end-to-end neural text-to-speech (TTS) system, MuLanTTS, designed for the Blizzard Challenge 2023. About 50 hours audiobook corpus for French TTS as hub task and another 2 hours speaker adaptation as spoke task are released to build synthesize voices for different test purposes including sentence, paragraph, homographs, list, etc. Building upon the DelightfulTTS, we adopt contextual and emotion encoders to fit the audiobook data to enrich the beyond sentences for longform prosody and dialogue expressiveness. Regarding the recording quality, we also apply denoise algorithms and long audio processing for both corpora. For hub task, only the 50-hours single speaker data is used for building TTS system, while for spoke task, a multi-style source model is used for target speaker fine tuning. MuLanTTS achieves mean scores of quality assessment 4.3 and 4.5 in the respective tasks, statistically comparable with natural speech while keeping good similarity according to similarity assessment. The excellent quality and similarity in this year's new and dense statistical evaluation show the effectiveness of our proposed system in both tasks.
